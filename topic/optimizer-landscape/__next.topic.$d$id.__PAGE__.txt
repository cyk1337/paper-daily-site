[Agarwal2020LRConfound]) reminds us: once HP-search budget is controlled, optimizer gaps shrink by more than half — a prerequisite every A/B report must honour.5:T4f4,≥70B production defaults to AdamW [Loshchilov2017AdamW] for what looks like 'most stable', but the real reason is the HP-transfer ecosystem. Lingle [Lingle2024muP] systematically validated muP LR transfer under AdamW from 40M→3B with failure modes documented; Yang [Yang2020TensorPrograms3]'s Tensor Programs III provides the theoretical basis; MiniCPM [Hu2024MiniCPM] turned it into a company-scale recipe. Every step in this chain is AdamW-specific — switch optimizers and the accumulated LR / warmup / weight-decay tables are void. Schlotthauer et al. [Schlotthauer2025Budget]'s head-to-head of AdamW/Lion/Sophia on budget pretraining finds AdamW the only baseline without negative outcomes — the other two are not worse in principle, they are more HP-sensitive. AdEMAMix [Pagliardini2024AdEMAMix] is the lowest-cost first-order upgrade: a β≈0.9999 slow EMA, fully HP-compatible, matched 1.3B loss with ~66% of the tokens. For ≥70B dense production this is the safest upgrade path. Replacing AdamW requires the new optimizer to build out comparable muP-style tooling (Ishikawa & Karakida [Ishikawa2023SecondOrdermuP]'s second-order muP is only a starting point) — not merely show lower loss at one scale. That is precisely what Muon/SOAP still lacks.6:T586,Shampoo [Gupta2018Shampoo]'s Kronecker preconditioner is a structured approximation to full-matrix AdaGrad; Morwani et al. [Morwani2024Shampoo] prove it approximates Gauss-Newton rather than the full Hessian — which explains why it is more stable than K-FAC on heavily overparameterized transformers. Shi et al. [Shi2023DistShampoo] fit Shampoo into PyTorch FSDP; Wang et al. [Wang2024Shampoo4bit] quantize the preconditioner to 4-bit for 8× memory reduction. What actually made Shampoo usable is SOAP [Vyas2024SOAP]: Vyas et al. run Adam in Shampoo's preconditioner eigenbasis, eliminating Shampoo's grafting pathology, cutting extra HPs from 4 to 1, and matching AdamW wall-clock. Lu et al. [Lu2025SOAPWhitening] further reinterpret SOAP as gradient whitening. Muon [Jordan2024Muon] takes the other route: approximate Shampoo's preconditioner with a 5-step Newton-Schulz orthogonalization — same O(d²) order but with a much smaller constant — applied only to 2D hidden weights while embedding/norm/head stay on AdamW. The NanoGPT speedrun from 5 min to 3.3 min (−34%) is what this hybrid delivers. The contrast: SOAP 'finishes Shampoo', Muon 'reduces Shampoo to its cheapest usable form'. For ≤30B new runs Muon has the lowest A/B cost; for research benchmarks and ≥7B long runs SOAP gives higher-quality signal — at the cost of a missing HP ecosystem that production teams must build themselves.7:T583,AdamW's optimizer state is 2P (m and v); a 7B model means ~28 GB — already a bottleneck on a single H100. This track has three representatives: Adam-mini [Zhang2024AdamMini] replaces per-param 1/√v with per-block LRs, halves memory, matches AdamW at 7B; Apollo [Zhu2024Apollo] goes further, swapping in a per-tensor scalar so state drops to near-SGD while still matching AdamW at 7B/13B; GaLore [Zhao2024GaLore] comes from the other direction, projecting gradients to a low-rank subspace then running Adam — single-GPU 7B pretrain works but long runs trail AdamW by 0.02–0.05 nats; Su et al.'s GaLore 2 [Su2025GaLore2] fixes this partially via dynamic rank and projection restarts. Adjacent work: AdaLomo [Lv2023AdaLomo] as a conceptual predecessor, Flora [Hao2024Flora] with LoRA-as-grad-compression, Natural GaLore [Das2024NaturalGaLore] using Fisher acceleration. The mechanical basis for ordering: Apollo does not change the training loop, introduces no new HP, and shows no quality gap at public 7B/13B — those three together equal 'default' for a product line. GaLore is the fallback when Apollo is not yet in the framework; Adam-mini is an intermediate waypoint. Contrast with Wang et al. [Wang2024Shampoo4bit]'s 4-bit Shampoo: 4-bit Shampoo exists so ≥7B SOAP/Shampoo can enter production; Apollo exists so first-order AdamW-quality can enter single-GPU fine-tune — the two do not compete.8:T4e6,Camp D's claim: most of the gap in public optimizer comparisons comes from LR-schedule and HP-search-budget confounds rather than algorithmic differences. Agarwal et al. [Agarwal2020LRConfound] already showed in 2020 that carefully tuning SGD's LR schedule to match Adam erases most of the adaptive advantage. Dahl et al. [Dahl2023AlgoPerf] engineered this observation into AlgoPerf — under matched HP budget, Nesterov/AdamW/Shampoo wall-clock gaps collapse by more than half. This applies to the 2024–2026 Muon / SOAP papers as well: any A/B report without controlled HP-search budget is noise from Camp D's view. The right response is not to ignore this line but to adopt it as experiment-design discipline — any production A/B must match HP-search budget and LR-schedule family across arms, or the report is not actionable. That said, the strong version of Camp D ('if data is right, optimizers don't matter') overshoots: Muon [Jordan2024Muon]'s −34% wall-clock on NanoGPT is under matched data and HP, not an LR trick; SOAP [Vyas2024SOAP]'s loss remains below AdamW under matched budget.
