Two independent but mutually consistent mechanistic threads deserve spelling out. First, token entropy. Code's identifier repetition, templated syntax and strict bracket pairing give it significantly lower bits/token than web—the downstream-variance data of Gadre et al. [Gadre2023Overtraining] is the clearest signal: at matched loss, code's downstream noise is ~30% lower than web. This is not 'code is easier' but 'code's gradient signal is steadier', equivalent to a lower effective learning rate. An immediate corollary: under a fixed LR schedule, adding code pulls the overall training dynamics toward smoother curvature—explaining why 15–25% simultaneously spares NL and helps reasoning. Second, induction heads. Olsson et al. [Olsson2022InductionHeads] locates the ICL phase transition at the moment an induction circuit emerges; code's repeat-token density, variable reuse and import dependencies are natural training signal for induction. [Madaan2022CodeReason] observe that code-pretrained LMs beat NL-pretrained ones on structured commonsense. The synergy/interference phase plot of Aghajanyan et al. [Aghajanyan2023SciMix] gives these two threads a quantitative envelope: below 10% synergy dominates, above 40% interference dominates—framing the code-ratio sweet spot exactly.
