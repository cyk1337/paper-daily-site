16:T627,The literature points to a concrete engineering conclusion: frontier pretraining after 2024 is no longer just “collect as much web text as possible, then lightly post-process it.” Instead, it treats mid-train as a distinct stage, spending an extra 10–30% compute to reshape the training distribution. Synthetic data here does not simply mean “the model writes more data.” It is a combinatorial toolkit: rephrase, seed→evol, teacher distillation, programmatic verifier, and filter-as-synthesis. The shared goal is not to replace the real distribution, but to move token budget from low-density web text toward regions with higher learning density under fixed compute. The main disagreements are threefold: whether synthetic data can substitute for large-scale real data, which domains keep benefiting from synthesis, and how to avoid collapse under recursive self-training. Public evidence supports a conservative but effective recipe: real data anchors the distribution, synthetic data amplifies scarce skills. Code and math scale further because executable verifiers make correctness filtering hard and reliable, allowing 10–50T-token regimes; natural-language reasoning lacks an equally strong verifier and often shows diminishing returns around 2–5T; agentic traces saturate even faster due to pattern collapse. The deciding factors are not a single “synthetic ratio,” but whether seeds come from real data, whether the verifier is stronger than the teacher, whether styles are diverse enough, and whether real data is accumulated rather than replaced.17:T661,A more stable reading is this: mid-train has become a standard component of frontier pretraining after 2024, typically consuming 10–30% of total training compute. Its role is not merely to “add some specialty data,” but to pull a base-pretrained model back toward high-value distributions such as code, math, reasoning, and long-context tasks.[Phi3Report][DeepSeekMath2024][Llama3Herd] Synthetic data should not be treated as a single recipe either. Public practice already spans at least five methods: rephrase, seed→evol, teacher distillation, programmatic verifier, and filter-as-synthesis.[WRAP2024][SelfInstruct2022][DataCompLM2024] What scales over time is not teacher style by itself, but verifier strength and distributional diversity. The evidence supports “accumulate real, do not replace real”: recursive pure synthesis loses tail modes and can trigger collapse, but this degradation is not inevitable when real data keeps accumulating.[MAD2023][CollapseInevitable2024] Domain differences matter. Code and math can scale synthetic tokens to roughly 10–50T because they have hard verifiers such as unit tests, symbolic checks, and numeric checks; natural-language reasoning lacks an equally strong correctness signal and often shows diminishing returns around 2–5T; agentic trajectories are even more prone to learning templates instead of capability and thus saturate faster.[DeepSeekMath2024][CodeContests2022] The engineering priority is therefore not maximizing the synthetic ratio, but controlling seed provenance, parallel styles, drop ratios, and how real and synthetic data are accumulated together.18:T605,The conclusion first: it is more accurate to view mid-train as “a second, targeted redistribution stage after pretraining” than as “adding a bit of specialty data.” That reading matches public recipes after 2024 more closely.[Phi3Report][DeepSeekMath2024][Llama3Herd] This stage usually happens after the base model already has general language competence. The goal is not to keep averaging over the web, but to spend the remaining compute on high-value, low-natural-frequency regions that matter more for downstream capability, such as code, math, long-context reasoning, and tool-use traces.[Phi3Report][DeepSeekMath2024] A separate stage is useful for two reasons. First, these distributions are small and would be diluted by massive web tokens if mixed from the start. Second, many synthetic pipelines only work reliably once a sufficiently strong teacher or base model already exists.[SelfInstruct2022][WRAP2024] This is why the core question is not simply whether synthetic data is used, but when it is inserted, how much compute it gets, and which distributions it targets. Public reports rarely disclose one universal ratio, but recipes such as Phi-3, DeepSeekMath, and Llama 3 suggest an engineering consensus: a dedicated steering stage using roughly 10–30% compute is often more stable than mixing all target distributions from the beginning, because it allows more aggressive sampling, filtering, and verifier loops without damaging the broad coverage of the base distribution.[Phi3Report][DeepSeekMath2024][Llama3Herd]19:T6be,Viewed together, public methods show that “synthetic data” is not one unified category but at least five distinct mechanisms.[WRAP2024][SelfInstruct2022][DataCompLM2024][Cosmopedia2024][DeepSeekMath2024] The first is rephrase: the content stays mostly the same, but the expression is rewritten into more regular styles such as textbook, wiki, or blog. The gain comes from noise compression and higher learning density per token.[WRAP2024][Cosmopedia2024] The second is seed→evol: start from a small set of real task seeds and let a teacher expand the task space; Self-Instruct is the canonical starting point.[SelfInstruct2022] The third is distillation: the teacher directly supplies a cleaner target distribution. The Phi line relies heavily on this, but its ceiling is constrained by teacher preference.[Textbooks2023][Phi3Report] The fourth is programmatic verifier: the teacher only proposes candidates, while unit tests, SymPy, or numeric checks decide what survives.
