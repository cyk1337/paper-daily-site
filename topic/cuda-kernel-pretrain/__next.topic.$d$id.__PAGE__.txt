[Dao2022FlashAttention] moved naive attention from HBM-bound to SMEM-tiled IO-aware, which was only possible because Ampere's SMEM was large enough and block-wise softmax can be numerically reassembled; Dao et al. [Dao2023FlashAttention2] tightened warp-level work partitioning for another ~2×; Shah et al. [Shah2024FA3] fully exploited Hopper's async tensor core + TMA + warp-specialization, pushing FP16 close to 75% of peak. This line was not hand-designed by algorithm folks — [Luo2024HopperDissect]'s microarchitectural numbers retroactively explain why it had to look this way. Meanwhile decode-side memory-boundness is effectively eternal: per-token FLOPs are small, per-token KV load is large, so Shazeer [Shazeer2019MQA] first collapsed KV heads to one, Ainslie et al. [Ainslie2023GQA] interpolated back to 8/4, and DeepSeek [DeepSeek2024V2]'s MLA compressed the state via a low-rank latent to an effective ~9 KB/token. All three are subtractions on the same bandwidth wall, not three independent ideas. Worth watching: Golden et al. [Fishman2024FP8Scale] hit that wall head-on at 2T tokens — Hopper only supports per-tensor scaling, and SwiGLU activation outliers accumulate into loss spikes. Their patch is 'Smooth-SwiGLU' plus FP8 Adam moments. Xi et al. [Xi2024COAT] then push FP8 into optimizer states and activations so memory finally approaches the theoretical half. Williams et al. [Williams2008Roofline]'s 18-year-old method is still right: compute arithmetic intensity = FLOPs / bytes, then classify on the peak-FLOPs × bandwidth roofline. Three buckets fall out: memory-bound (GEMV, decode attention, RMSNorm), compute-bound (long-prefill GEMM, FA3 forward), and latency-bound (small-kernel chains, Python overhead, NCCL launches). The recipe then becomes mechanical: memory-bound wants fusion / register reuse / bf16→fp8 to cut bytes (while guarding against the numerics leakage in [Golden2024FAStability]); compute-bound wants tile-size tuning, warp specialization, async copy (see [CUTLASS3], [Shah2024FA3]); latency-bound wants CUDA Graph launch batching or Triton ([Tillet2019Triton]) to reduce kernel count. Pope et al. [Pope2022ScalingInference] quantify the decode/prefill memory↔compute crossover as a function of batch × sequence length and remain the canonical serving-side playbook.
