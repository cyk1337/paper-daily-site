4:T686,The core objective of subquadratic sequence modeling is to overcome the $O(L^2)$ compute and memory bottlenecks of Transformer self-attention, enabling linear or near-linear scaling for long-context scenarios. Over the past few years, this field has evolved along two primary technical trajectories. The first is State Space Models (SSMs), represented by S4 and Mamba, which achieve efficient sequence compression through the discretization of continuous-time systems and input-dependent gating (Selective Scan). The second is linear attention variants, such as RWKV and RetNet, which replace the Softmax operation with additive recurrences or exponential decay, unifying parallel training with $O(1)$ state updates during inference. However, compressing an entire sequence into a fixed-dimensional hidden state $d_{\text{state}}$ inevitably introduces an information bottleneck. Multiple independent studies have shown that pure SSMs or pure linear attention models exhibit systematic deficits in tasks requiring exact match, associative recall, and in-context learning (ICL). To address this, the current engineering consensus has shifted towards hybrid architectures and knowledge distillation. For instance, Jamba interleaves Transformer and Mamba layers to recover exact copying capabilities while maintaining subquadratic complexity. Meanwhile, distilling attention matrices from pretrained Transformers directly into SSM hidden states has significantly reduced the cold-start training cycles for subquadratic models. This report outlines the theoretical evolution, hardware constraints, and pragmatic trade-offs between expressivity and efficiency in this domain.5:T51a,The core mechanism of subquadratic models is replacing the linearly growing KV Cache in Transformers with a fixed-dimensional hidden state $x_t \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{state}}}$. The Structured State Space Duality (SSD) theory proposed by Dao et al. [Dao2024Mamba2] shows that this compression is mathematically equivalent to approximating a full-rank attention matrix with a semiseparable matrix. However, this approximation hits hard physical limits when processing discrete symbolic logic. Independent tests by Grazzi et al. [Grazzi2024MambaICL] and Park et al. [Park2024CanMamba] demonstrate that Mamba systematically lags behind Transformers on in-context learning tasks requiring exact copying (e.g., induction heads) and dense entity binding. The reason is mechanical: attention performs $O(1)$-distance addressing directly via dot products, whereas SSMs must "squeeze" historical information into $d_{\text{state}}$. When a sequence contains numerous low-frequency entities that require exact differentiation, the state vector suffers from severe feature interference. Fu et al. [Fu2022H3] observed similar upper bounds in language modeling capabilities early on with the H3 model. Therefore, attempting to completely replace Transformers with pure SSMs for logical reasoning tasks is impractical.6:T4d3,The elegance of SSMs and linear attention (e.g., RWKV [Peng2023RWKV], RetNet [Sun2023RetNet]) lies in their duality: using CNN mode (or parallel scan) during training for $O(L)$ high parallelism, and switching to RNN mode during inference for $O(1)$ state updates. Although the Selective Scan introduced by Gu et al. [Gu2023Mamba] breaks traditional convolutional parallelism, it achieves efficient computation in SRAM via hardware-aware parallel prefix sums. However, in actual deployment, the RNN mode is not without cost. During autoregressive generation, while memory footprint drops from $O(L)$ to $O(1)$, each token generation requires loading the massive hidden state matrix (e.g., Mamba's $d_{\text{model}} \times d_{\text{state}}$) from HBM. Lahoti et al. [Lahoti2026Mamba3] point out that when $d_{\text{state}}$ is large, this per-token state read quickly hits the GPU's memory bandwidth bottleneck. In contrast, Transformer KV Caches, deeply optimized by FlashAttention and PagedAttention, often yield higher actual generation throughput on short-to-medium sequences than unoptimized SSMs. This explains why in production environments, purely chasing $O(1)$ complexity does not always translate to end-to-end latency gains.7:T44e,Given the expressivity deficits of pure SSMs and the complexity issues of pure Attention, the industry has rapidly converged on hybrid architectures. Jamba, proposed by Lieber et al. [Lieber2024Jamba], exemplifies this route: by interleaving Transformer and Mamba layers at a 1:7 ratio, the model retains the exact associative recall provided by Attention (solving the induction heads problem) while shrinking the overall KV Cache by nearly 90%. Wang et al. [Wang2026InfoMamba] further explored attention-free hybrid interfaces in InfoMamba. Furthermore, pretraining a 100B-parameter SSM from scratch is highly risky and expensive. Bick et al. [Bick2024Distill] validated a more stable path: distilling the "quadratic attention knowledge" of pretrained Transformers directly into subquadratic models. By matching the outputs of hidden states and attention matrices, the model can inherit the original model's ICL capabilities in very few steps. This paradigm of "architectural downgrading + knowledge distillation" has become the standard practice for migrating existing LLMs to long-context scenarios.8:T596,An executable checklist:
1. **Adopt Hybrid Architectures**: Do not use pure SSMs when designing long-context models. Following [Lieber2024Jamba], inserting 1 standard Attention layer every 4-8 SSM layers is sufficient to recover exact recall while reducing KV Cache by over 80%.
2. **Beware the Memory Bandwidth Trap**: Do not evaluate inference latency based solely on FLOPs. When $d_{\text{state}}$ is large, the memory bandwidth overhead of loading hidden states per token in RNN mode is massive [Lahoti2026Mamba3]; for short sequences (<2K), optimized Transformers are often faster.
3. **Leverage Distillation for Cold Starts**: Do not train 100B-parameter SSMs from random initialization. Using methods from [Bick2024Distill] to distill open-source Transformer weights into SSMs skips the lengthy learning phase for basic grammar and world knowledge.
4. **Define Task Boundaries**: For tasks requiring dense entity binding (e.g., SQL generation, long-context needle-in-a-haystack), expect pure Mamba to drop 15-20 pp compared to scale-matched Transformers [Grazzi2024MambaICL]; however, SSMs excel at semantic compression tasks like summarization and style rewriting.
