4:T47b,Pre-2022 pretraining treated data mixture as lore: the Pile's 22 domain weights, The Stack's hand-picked ratios, C4's single-source mix were all heuristic. Between 2023 and 2025, DoReMi [Xie2023DoReMi], RegMix [Liu2024RegMix] and ODM [Albalak2024ODM] reframed it as a constrained optimization: domain weight w ∈ Δ^K, objective = downstream loss or worst-case group loss. In parallel, the engineering disclosures of Llama 3 [MetaLlama32024] and DataComp-LM [Li2024DCLM] showed that the mixture is not a single static vector but a curriculum—web-heavy early to stabilize the loss surface, capability-heavy late to upsample code/math/reasoning. This report treats data mixture as a serious engineering problem: dedup and effective-count accounting, domain granularity, ratio-search method choice (heuristic vs DoReMi vs RegMix vs online), curriculum staging, and ratio-by-scale interactions. We end with an executable default and name the axes the community still disputes: whether DoReMi-style 2× overhead is worth it, whether online methods are stable, and whether ratio degenerates to a secondary knob when quality filters are strong enough.5:T596,The proper formulation: given K domains and weight w ∈ Δ^K, sampling probability p_k(w) = w_k, minimize a downstream proxy L_down(θ(w)) where θ(w) is the model trained with w as sampling distribution. Every engineering disagreement reduces to three questions: what is L_down, how do we estimate it cheaply, and does the small-scale w* transfer. DoReMi [Xie2023DoReMi] picks worst-case group loss (Group DRO), reasoning that 'rare domains matter more than proportional'; its w* is learned via a proxy of the same architecture, one size smaller. DoGE [Fan2023DoGE] offers a principled patch: replace worst-case loss with each domain's marginal contribution to held-out generalization, which assigns saner weights to small-but-effective domains like math and code. RegMix [Liu2024RegMix] takes another route entirely: drop the DRO objective, treat (w, L_down) as tabular regression samples over 512 1M-param runs, fit a LightGBM, and argmin the predictor. Data Mixing Laws [Ye2024DataMixLaws] goes further with a parametric closed form (one exponential per domain) allowing w* extrapolation across N and D. All four share one assumption: small-scale w* approximates large-scale w*, i.e. ratio × scale is roughly invariant. It holds in [Liu2024RegMix]'s 1M→1B and [Ye2024DataMixLaws]'s cross-scale experiments, but has not been stressed in the >30B or >10-domain regime—a load-bearing assumption that still deserves caution.6:T56d,Llama 3 [MetaLlama32024] pretraining is explicitly two-phase: roughly the first 85% of compute runs a steady web-heavy mixture, the final 10–15% enters a mid-train / annealing stage where code is upsampled ~3–5×, math/reasoning similarly, and LR cosine-decays into the floor. MiniCPM [Hu2024MiniCPM] reproduces the same template at ~1B scale: the decay stage and the mixture shift are synchronized events, and downstream MMLU/GSM8K show a step-up across that boundary. The mechanistic question: why not run the capability-heavy mix end-to-end? Three interlocking reasons. First, web-heavy early training stabilizes the loss surface—narrow high-quality domains like math/code have large gradient variance near random init and can destabilize early training. Second, the repetition law [Muennighoff2023Repeat] caps scarce domains at roughly four epochs of fresh-token-equivalent value; piling them into the front stage burns that budget before the model has converged enough to exploit it. Third, in the annealing tail gradient norms fall, so per-step value extracted from information-dense tokens is highest—this is precisely what the MiniCPM decay-stage ablation shows. Curriculum is not free: it lifts mixture optimization from 1D (a single w) to 2D (a trajectory w(t)), enlarging the search space. Extending DoReMi / RegMix to stage-wise search is an open engineering direction.7:T4a8,The 7B controlled study in DCLM [Li2024DCLM] delivers an uncomfortable result: a strong fastText classifier plus perplexity filter yields ~3–6 pp on MMLU, the same order as careful mixture optimization. FineWeb-Edu [Penedo2024FineWeb] reproduces this on the web side—an edu-classifier pass lifts MMLU by an amount comparable to a full DoReMi run. Phi-1 [Gunasekar2023Textbooks] pushes the route to its extreme: trained purely on textbook-quality plus synthetic data with barely any ratio discussion, it reaches 50%+ on HumanEval. Two readings of this evidence. Reading A ('ratio is secondary'): filters reshape the effective token distribution so drastically that mixture weights degenerate into a secondary knob. Reading B (more defensible): filters and ratios compete for the same improvement budget—mixture tuning on unfiltered Common Crawl buys 5 pp, on DCLM-Baseline maybe only 1–2 pp, and filter+ratio is not 5+3=8 but closer to max(5, 3) plus a small residual. This explains why a recipe like Llama 3 [MetaLlama32024], already running aggressive filters, invests in curriculum rather than another round of ratio search—the marginal return has migrated from ratio to schedule.8:T512,Discussing domain ratios before dedup is largely meaningless. D4 [Tirumala2023D4] supplies two hard numbers: document-level dedup + SemDeDup yields ~20% training speedup; more importantly, the near-duplicate rate in Common Crawl means a nominal '70% web' mixture may have an effective web share of only 40–50%, with math/code proportionally diluted. Dolma's [Soldaini2024Dolma] public pipeline shows 3T raw tokens collapsing to roughly 1.6T effective tokens after MinHash + fuzzy dedup; any weight assignment has to be recalibrated over that post-dedup distribution. The repetition law [Muennighoff2023Repeat] clamps this from the other side: for scarce domains (math, specialized code), ≤4 epochs are roughly as good as fresh tokens, with returns collapsing past 16. Together these yield a hard rule: the ceiling on domain k in the mixture is approximately 4 × (effective_tokens_k / total_train_tokens × current_ratio). This ceiling is frequently violated: a decision to 'upsample math to 8%' on a 1T-token run with only 10B effective math tokens implies 16 epochs, past the ROI knee identified in [Muennighoff2023Repeat].
