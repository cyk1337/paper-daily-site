16:T45c,FA1 [Dao2022FA1] in 2022 reframed attention as an IO-aware op: tiling + online softmax reduced HBM I/O from O(L²) to O(L²/√M), giving 3–15× speedups at seq ≥ 1K over vanilla PyTorch attention. FA2 [Dao2023FA2] in 2023 kept the algorithm and rebuilt the scheduling — warp-level work partitioning, parallelism along the seq dimension, removal of redundant softmax-rescale FLOPs — lifting A100 utilization from ~30% to 70%+. FA3 [Shah2024FA3] in 2024 went H100-native with warp specialization, TMA async copies, and FP8 matmul, reaching ~75% of BF16 peak and ~1.2 PFLOPs/s in FP8 — more than 2× FA2-BF16. In parallel, FlashDecoding [Hong2023FlashDec] and FlashInfer [Ye2024FlashInfer] repurposed the training-shape kernel for decode; FlexAttention [Dong2024Flex] on top of Triton [Tillet2019Triton] gave ALiBi / SWA / MoA / soft-mask variants FA-class throughput. This report lays out the five-year trajectory, the 2026 default choices across training, serving and variants, and the live disagreement with the SSM / linear-attention camp on whether attention itself is still the right thing to optimize.17:T42f,By 2026 the attention-kernel question is no longer 'can it go faster' but 'at which layer is optimization worth it'. FA1 [Dao2022FA1] solved the memory-bound bottleneck via tiling + online softmax, cutting HBM I/O from O(L²) to O(L²/√M); FA2 [Dao2023FA2] pushed utilization past 70% through work partitioning; FA3 [Shah2024FA3] reached ~75% of H100 BF16 peak and ~1.2 PFLOPs/s in FP8 via warp specialization + TMA + FP8. The training-side debate is largely settled: BF16 FA3 is default on H100/H200, and FP8 FA3 requires numerical ablations in the style of [Golden2024FAStable]. On the serving side, FlashDecoding [Hong2023FlashDec] and FlashInfer [Ye2024FlashInfer] are production-grade — rolling a custom serving attention kernel rarely pencils out. Variants (SWA, ALiBi, MoA, soft masks) should default to FlexAttention [Dong2024Flex], which covers ~80% of use cases; reserve CUDA for the last 20%. The SSM / linear-attention alternative still trails by 2–5 pp on retrieval and ICL at 8B scale [Waleffe2024Mamba] and wins reliably only in hybrid architectures.18:T5d3,The three FA generations share one assumption: 'attention is bottlenecked by memory, not FLOPs'. FA1 [Dao2022FA1] turned this into a provable I/O lower bound — tiling + online softmax avoids ever materializing the L×L intermediate, cutting HBM I/O from O(L²) to O(L²/√M), with 15× measured speedup at seq=8K and bitwise-comparable numerics. The idea rhymes with O(log n) memory attention in [Rabe2021Memory], but FA1 delivered the first production-grade CUDA kernel — the industrialization watershed. When FA2 [Dao2023FA2] took over, attention was already half compute-bound; the remaining gap was scheduling. Softmax rescale was hoisted out of the inner loop, parallelism added the seq dimension on top of batch×head, and warp-level load balance was hand-tuned per block; A100 BF16 utilization jumped from ~35% to 70%+. FA3 [Shah2024FA3] is purely a Hopper story: producer/consumer warp specialization decouples TMA async loads from MMA compute, and the ~600-cycle TMA latency measured in [Luo2024HopperBench] is exactly absorbed by MMA issue. FP8 E4M3/E5M2 [Micikevicius2022FP8] with block-wise scaling lands in attention at <0.1% loss drift, doubling throughput from ~740 TFLOPs/s (BF16) to ~1.2 PFLOPs/s. Often overlooked: the cumulative FA1→FA3 speedup does not come from one 'revolutionary' idea but from three separate layers of scheduling/hardware adaptation stacked on each other — every generation's 2× required fully metabolizing that era's GPU micro-architecture.19:T5ad,FA1/2/3 parallelism is engineered for training shapes — batch×head×seq is big enough to keep SMs saturated. Online decode is the opposite: batch=1, q_len=1, kv_len=32K–1M, and the working set collapses into a skinny matmul where training-shape kernels deliver <20% SM occupancy. [Hong2023FlashDec] fixes this with a simple but essential trick: chunk the KV along seq into 128/256-token blocks, let each SM handle a chunk independently, then combine local softmaxes via log-sum-exp reduction. Not a new algorithm — just FA1's online softmax extended once more along the KV dimension — but it immediately yields 2–4× decode throughput. FlashInfer [Ye2024FlashInfer] rolls this together with paged KV cache, GQA [Ainslie2023GQA] / MQA [Shazeer2019MQA] head grouping, prefix cache and speculative-decoding verify kernels into a unified C++/Python library; as of 2024-Q4 it is vLLM's default attention backend. The blunt conclusion: in 2026 the ROI on hand-writing serving attention kernels is mostly negative — unless you are chasing a mask shape FlashInfer does not yet cover. Another underrated shift: GQA/MQA has graduated from 'optimization' to 'assumption'. FA3's decode path and FlashInfer defaults both presume KV heads ≤ 1/4 of query heads; under that premise KV-cache bandwidth is no longer the bottleneck, and remaining attention-kernel headroom lives in prefix cache and speculative-decoding verification, not in the core matmul.1a:T58d,Before FlexAttention [Dong2024Flex] the ecosystem had an awkward gap: FA covered only causal / full / fixed-window masks, and anything else (ALiBi, sliding-window variants, MoA, prompt soft masks, document masks) forced a choice between (a) naive PyTorch attention at 10–30× slower, or (b) several hundred lines of hand-written CUDA. Works like [Hassani2024Neighborhood] proved 'FA on variant' was feasible at the kernel level, but each variant needed its own kernel — not a scalable engineering cost. FlexAttention's move: express the score modification (ALiBi bias, log-scale, prompt-mask addition) as `score_mod(score, b, h, q_idx, kv_idx) -> score`, and the mask as a `mask_mod` returning bool. torch.compile inlines both Python callables into a FA-style Triton fused kernel; the first call pays compile overhead, afterward the gap to hand-written kernels sits at 5–15%. Measured ALiBi reaches 92% of FA2, sliding window 89%, document mask 85%. This works only because of Triton's [Tillet2019Triton] block-level DSL — without its automatic shared-memory management and swizzling, the 'Python callable in, fused CUDA kernel out' pipeline would not exist. Consequence: in 2026 the default for variant attention is FlexAttention; hand-written CUDA on FA3 is reserved for two cases — a mask pattern FlexAttention does not yet support, or a production-critical path where the 10% gap translates to real dollars.1b:T5cd,Two topics deserve to be read together. First, FP8 attention numerics. FA3 FP8 [Shah2024FA3] shows <0.1% per-step loss deviation on 10B-scale LM pretraining on H100 — looks risk-free. But [Golden2024FAStable]'s systematic audit shows certain rescale orderings amplify the BF16-vs-reference gap to ~1e-3, and FP8 makes it worse once E4M3's dynamic range kicks in. The E4M3/E5M2 spec [Micikevicius2022FP8] requires E4M3 forward (higher precision), E5M2 backward (wider range), and FA3 FP8's block-wise scaling follows suit. Production rule: FP8 training must run a 2–5B-step BF16 control and monitor per-layer max|QK| — exactly where massive activations [Sun2024MassiveAct] will overflow FP8. Second, whether SSM / linear attention should replace attention. RetNet [Sun2023RetNet] claimed the triple benefit in 2023; [Waleffe2024Mamba]'s controlled 8B/3.5T experiments give the cooler answer: short-context LM loss matches, but in-context learning and retrieval lag by 2–5 pp, and a hybrid with ~1/6 attention layers closes the gap. Meaning: evidence for 'full attention replacement' remains thin in 2026, but 'attention layer share drops from 100% to ~16%' is verified reality. So the payoff from attention-kernel optimization does not evaporate in hybrid models — it concentrates on the ~16% of layers still using attention, and those are exactly the layers that must run full-precision long-context. 3. Do not hand-write serving attention kernels; pick FlashInfer [Ye2024FlashInfer] (or frameworks that integrate it, e.g. vLLM / TensorRT-LLM). In 2026 the ROI of rolling your own is usually negative.
4. For variants (ALiBi, SWA, MoA, document mask, soft prompt mask) default to FlexAttention [Dong2024Flex]; fall back to hand-written CUDA only when a 5–15% gap on a production critical path burns real money.

6. Pick head_dim from 64/96/128/192/256 where pre-built fast paths exist; for non-standard dims benchmark FlexAttention first, only then consider hand-writing.
7. Do not put 'replace attention with SSM' on the 2026 roadmap: [Waleffe2024Mamba]'s retrieval/ICL gap persists. If you must bet, pick a hybrid (~1/6 attention layers), and those 16% stay on FA3 + FlashInfer.
