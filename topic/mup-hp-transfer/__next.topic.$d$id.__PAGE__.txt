4:T5c5,In 2026, hyperparameter (HP) transfer for LLM pretraining has evolved beyond the binary debate of 'to use µP or not', fracturing into four distinct routes strictly divided by engineering scenarios. The first route is the industrialization of theory: for greenfield pretraining stacks, Complete-P combined with u-µP and depth-µP forms the new default, suppressing learning rate (LR) drift under 15% across width, depth, and fp8 precision dimensions. The second route relies on empirical formulas: for stable Standard Parameterization (SP) stacks, the engineering ROI of force-migrating to µP is negative; leveraging joint scaling laws published by Cerebras and DeepSeek, target-scale LR can be predicted with <10% error using minimal proxy runs. The third route is localized Bayesian search: for HPs outside µP's theoretical scope (e.g., weight decay, β₂, dropout), CARBS offers a cost-aware Pareto-frontier search, squeezing out residual performance gains with 10-20 small-scale runs. The fourth route is the persistent brute-force reality of the industry: as shown by the Llama and PaLM series, manual tuning based on intuition still scales to 500B, though often at the cost of 10%-20% wasted compute. This report delineates the boundaries of these four routes, arguing that HP transfer is fundamentally about matching tools to the infrastructure lifecycle, and emphasizes that any µP deployment lacking a coord check validation should be treated as void engineering.5:T568,The foundational µP theory established by Yang et al. [Yang2022muP] guarantees a well-defined feature learning limit as width approaches infinity via abc-parametrization. However, empirical studies by Lingle [Lingle2024EmpiricalMuP] showed that basic µP suffers significant LR drift when integrated with modern LLM components. Cerebras Research [Cerebras2024CompleteP] identified the mechanism behind this conflict: components like QK-Norm, tied embeddings, and residual scaling break the original activation variance conservation. By deriving independent width scaling rules for each component, Complete-P suppresses LR drift back to <10% at scales above 1B.

The µP puzzle has also been completed across precision and depth dimensions. Blake et al. [Blake2024UMUP] introduced u-µP to solve low-precision training pain points: in fp8, activation and gradient dynamic ranges are extremely narrow. u-µP enforces unit RMS across all tensors via unit-scaling, completely decoupling LR sweeps from fp8 overflow rescue. Simultaneously, Bordelon et al. [Bordelon2023InfiniteDepth] extended transferability to the depth dimension (depth-µP). For greenfield pretraining stacks, the combination of Complete-P + u-µP + depth-µP requires only 1-2 weeks of one-shot engineering, yielding <15% HP drift across width × depth × fp8. This is the only rational choice for new infrastructure.6:T552,For Standard Parameterization (SP) stacks that are already stable and operational, the engineering ROI of forcibly refactoring low-level code to support µP is typically negative. Here, empirical formulas offer a highly competitive alternative. Dey et al. [Dey2023CerebrasGPT] published the training details of Cerebras-GPT, proving that a simple LR ∝ 1/√width empirical formula keeps errors under 10% across the 111M to 13B range. DeepSeek-AI [Bi2024DeepSeekLLM] pushed this further by fitting LR and Batch Size as independent variables jointly, providing closed-form relations to deduce target-scale HPs directly from small proxy runs, reducing errors by ~2× compared to 1D formulas at the 7B scale.

The core advantage of empirical formulas is their 'zero-invasiveness'—requiring no changes to model code or optimizer states. However, this convenience rests on strict assumptions: the architecture and training schedule must remain absolutely locked. McLeish et al. [McLeish2025Gemstones] explicitly warned that scaling laws are highly sensitive to HPs and model shapes (aspect ratios); changing the aspect ratio or LR schedule can drastically drift the fitted compute-optimal token:param ratio from 20 to 60. Therefore, empirical formulas are strictly applicable only in the production phase where 'architecture is frozen, focus is purely on scaling'.7:T560,Even if Complete-P perfectly solves LR transfer across width and depth, numerous HPs (e.g., weight decay, AdamW's β₂, dropout rate) have near-zero width dependence and fall outside µP's theoretical scope. CARBS, proposed by Fetterman et al. [Fetterman2024CARBS], fills this void. As an industrialized version of cost-aware Bayesian Optimization, CARBS strictly searches the Gaussian process along the cost × loss Pareto frontier. Empirical results show that with just 10-20 small-scale runs, CARBS optimizes the remaining HPs to near-optimal at the 7B scale. µP handles LR, CARBS handles the rest; the two are mathematically orthogonal.

Furthermore, the dynamic relationship between time (training tokens) and Batch Size remains a blind spot for traditional transfer. Filatov et al. [Filatov2024TimeTransfer]'s Time-Transfer study indicates that optimal LR decays by T^{-α} as data volume increases, a rule that must be superimposed on µP. Meanwhile, the Surge phenomenon observed by Li et al. [Li2024Surge] shows that optimal LR is non-monotonic with Batch Size in certain regimes, breaking traditional linear scaling rules. Finally, when introducing any new optimizer (e.g., Lion [Chen2023Lion]), because its update rules alter gradient scaling behaviors, a dedicated coord check must be independently derived and verified; otherwise, transfer will inevitably fail.8:T424,An executable checklist (in priority order):
