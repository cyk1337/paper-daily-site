What actually decides whether a model uses its context are three upstream choices — data distribution (burstiness / skewed Zipfian, Chan et al. [Chan2022DataDist]), pretraining packing (In-Context Pretraining, Shi et al. [Shi2023InContextPretraining]; fewer truncations, Ding et al. [Ding2024FewerTrunc]), and the long-document continual-pretrain recipe (Fu et al. [Fu2024DataEngineering]; Xiong et al. [Xiong2023EffectiveLongCtx]). These upstream choices directly control whether the induction-head circuit (Olsson et al. [Olsson2022InductionHeads]) trains and transfers across documents. This report partitions the 2020–2024 literature into four camps — positional encoding, data recipe, packing engineering, mechanistic — and argues, cross-paper, for a more robust allocation: 10% positional, 60% data, 30% packing.5:T51f,From 2021–2023, long context was treated almost by default as a positional-encoding problem. Since RoPE [Su2021RoFormer], the community iterated along one line: PI [Chen2023PI] linear interpolation, YaRN [Peng2023YaRN] NTK-aware band scaling, LongRoPE [Ding2024LongRoPE] pushing context to 2M tokens. This work matters — position is a necessary condition; without a sane RoPE base, attention diverges numerically past training length. Yet when Hsieh et al. [Hsieh2024RULER] test mainstream '128K models' with 13 controlled tasks in 2024, the verdict is sharp: most collapse around 32K effective length; perplexity may look flat but task capability is gone. Karpinska et al. [Karpinska2024NoCha] push further on book-length reasoning: even Gemini-1.5-Pro-1M lands near 50%. Contrast this with Fu et al. [Fu2024DataEngineering]'s ablation: positional-only changes leave Llama-2-4K→128K flat on NIAH; add long-document upsampling at correct ratios with preserved domain mix and 5B continual tokens match GPT-4-128K. Xiong et al. [Xiong2023EffectiveLongCtx] independently replicate the direction. The problem gets re-framed: the main variable is data distribution and packing, not positional encoding. The RoPE lineage has effectively ceded priority to a 10% positional / 60% data / 30% packing split by 2024.6:T5c8,Two widely cited but rarely co-read papers complete the causal chain. Chan et al. [Chan2022DataDist] show on controlled synthetic classification that ICL emergence correlates weakly with scale but strongly with three distributional properties: burstiness (same-class clustering within a sequence), skewed Zipfian (long tails), and many rare classes. Flatten them and ICL disappears; exaggerate them and small models ICL. Olsson et al. [Olsson2022InductionHeads] then supply the circuit fingerprint on real transformers — induction heads: 'find the prior occurrence of the current token and copy what followed'. Their phase transition coincides with the ICL phase transition on training curves, reproduced on 1-layer toys and on Anthropic models. Read together: ICL is not magic. It is a specific circuit summoned by specific data statistics. Shi et al. [Shi2023InContextPretraining] engineer the chain — retrieval-cluster related documents adjacent in packed sequences, manufacturing burstiness within pretrain windows (same entities, terms, citations recur), so induction heads transfer across documents. At matched compute, +8% ICL, +15% long-doc QA, +16% RAG — essentially giving the circuit the statistical structure it should have seen all along. Staniszewski et al. [Staniszewski2023StructuredPacking] take a different clustering route and replicate similar magnitudes on 128K synthetic tasks (+8 pp retrieval, +15 pp multi-hop) — independent evidence, not a re-run.7:T596,Treating packing as a pure engineering detail is expensive. Ding et al. [Ding2024FewerTrunc] make a simple observation in an industrial pipeline: traditional concat + hard split makes ~30% of training tokens come from truncated documents, so the model sees many semantically incomplete fragments. Best-fit bin-packing lifts HellaSwag / MMLU / GSM8K by 0.5–1.5 pp simultaneously with a small throughput gain. This is independent of Shi et al. [Shi2023InContextPretraining]: one says 'put related documents together', the other says 'don't split a document in half'. Stacked, they are the default 2024 packing recipe. On data recipes, Fu et al. [Fu2024DataEngineering] and Xiong et al. [Xiong2023EffectiveLongCtx] agree but independently: the former ablates at 5B continual tokens and pins 'long-document upsample with preserved domain mix' as the primary factor; the latter delivers stable 32K on Llama-2-70B with +3–7 pp on long-tail tasks.
