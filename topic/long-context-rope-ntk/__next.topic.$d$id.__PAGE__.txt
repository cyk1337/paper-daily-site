4:T44e,RoPE [Su2021RoPE] rotates (q,k) at per-dim frequencies θ_i = base^(-2i/d), inducing relative dependence. What actually made long context production-ready is the chain of fixes addressing one root cause — low-frequency dims entering rotation angles unseen during training. PI [Chen2023PI] compresses positions uniformly; NTK-aware [bloc972023NTK] argues high-freq dims must not be compressed and derives a base^(d/(d-2)) selective dilation; YaRN [Peng2023YaRN] fuses the two into a per-dim ramp and adds an attention temperature 1/sqrt(1+log s); LongRoPE [Ding2024LongRoPE] uses evolutionary search for non-uniform per-dim scales, pushing Llama-2-7B to 2M; ABF (Llama 3 [Dubey2024Llama3], Llama-Long [Xiong2023LongLlama]) simply pretrains with base=500000 plus a 6-stage curriculum. By 2026 the stack has settled: new pretrains use ABF + curriculum; retrofits ≤128K use YaRN; ≥512K use LongRoPE; evaluation goes through RULER [Hsieh2024RULER] and LongBench [Bai2023LongBench], not PPL. This report walks the six-step path, its mechanisms, trade-offs, a production recipe, and the gaps still open.5:T6b7,RoPE [Su2021RoPE] rotates (q,k) at dim-i frequency θ_i = base^(−2i/d). With base=10000 and d=128, the highest-frequency dim has a period around 2π (a few tokens) while the lowest-frequency dim can reach hundreds of thousands of tokens. Within a train length T, the low-frequency dims only sweep the arc [0, T·θ_min); the rest of the circle is never sampled. At inference length 4T the model sees rotation angles it has never encountered — the mechanical root cause of extrapolation collapse. Men et al. [Men2024RoPEBase] formalize this: the RoPE base imposes an upper bound on the learnable effective context; base=10000 caps at ~8K and base=500000 at ~200K. ABF's base=500000 is a direct consequence of that bound, not a lucky number.

This also resolves the ALiBi [Press2021ALiBi] paradox. ALiBi looks stable on naive next-token PPL extrapolation, but Zhong et al. [Zhong2024AttnRoPE] show from an attention-distribution view that its linear bias monotonically decays far-range attention toward zero, yielding a shorter effective receptive field on retrieval-style long-context tasks than YaRN/RoPE. NoPE results from Kazemnejad et al. [Kazemnejad2023PE] fit the same pattern: elegant at toy scale, not reproducible at real LLM scale where attention-head spectra are far richer than toy tasks.

A third detail overlooked before YaRN is attention entropy. When context stretches by factor s, softmax's normalizer grows and the contribution of long-range logits to the distribution compresses. Peng et al. [Peng2023YaRN] introduce a 1/√(1+log s) temperature specifically to offset this entropy collapse — the crucial step from 'position encoding problem' to 'position encoding + attention geometry problem'.6:T645,PI [Chen2023PI] compresses m to m·(T_train/T_target), dragging every rotation angle back into the trained domain at the cost of squeezing high-frequency dims. bloc97's [bloc972023NTK] key insight is 'only compress the low end': base ← base · s^(d/(d−2)) leaves high-freq θ_i almost untouched. YaRN [Peng2023YaRN] merges PI and NTK into a per-dim ramp log-linearly interpolating between high and low frequencies, and adds the attention-temperature term — a double alignment of 'first frequencies, then softmax shape'. LongRoPE [Ding2024LongRoPE] goes further: the smooth formula is suboptimal, different heads/dims need genuinely different scales; evolutionary search over 96 dims plus 1000-step fine-tune reaches 2M.

The pretrain-time path ABF takes the opposite philosophy: if low-freq dims were never trained, train them once during pretraining. Xiong et al. [Xiong2023LongLlama] move Llama-2's base from 10000 to 500000 and continue-pretrain to 32K. Dubey et al. [Dubey2024Llama3] extend this into a 6-stage curriculum (8K→16K→32K→64K→128K) at ~5% tokens per stage. Young et al. [Young2024Yi] reproduce on Yi-200K. Gemini 1.5 [GeminiTeam2024]'s 1M–10M regime is almost certainly ABF combined with LongRoPE-style search, not any single path.

The table below places the six steps and their rivals in one coordinate system. Note the asymmetries: PI is global scale, the rest are per-dim; NTK/YaRN are closed-form, LongRoPE is searched; ABF needs no inference-time remapping, everyone else does. These structural differences set their capability frontiers at the 32K / 128K / 1M bands.7:T5d8,Mapping the pool's contradicts labels and the anchor papers' implicit oppositions gives four production camps. Camp A: pretrain-time ABF is the only clean path — low-freq dims never went OOD, so no interpolation is needed [Xiong2023LongLlama][Dubey2024Llama3][Young2024Yi]. Camp B: at ≤128K, retrofit sits within 3 pp of ABF on RULER; YaRN + 400 FT steps is saturated [Peng2023YaRN][Chen2023LongLoRA][Pal2023Giraffe]. Camp C: at ≥512K, per-dim search is unavoidable and smooth formulas have a visible gap at 1M [Ding2024LongRoPE][GeminiTeam2024]. Camp D questions the entire PI/NTK/YaRN interpolation lineage, arguing for LM-Infinite-style masking zero-shot [Han2023LMInfinite] or architecture swaps (RetNet [Sun2023RetNet], LongNet [Ding2023LongNet]) to sidestep position encoding.

2024–2025 evidence cuts these boundaries fairly cleanly. Once RULER [Hsieh2024RULER] appeared, non-RoPE paths essentially fell out at 128K multi-hop — methods from Han et al. [Han2023LMInfinite] and Sun et al. [Sun2023RetNet] pass single-needle but collapse on multi-hop aggregation. The attention-spectrum analysis by Zhong et al. [Zhong2024AttnRoPE] gives the mechanical reason: non-RoPE paths suffer premature long-range entropy collapse. On the other side, Gao, Li et al. [Li2025Haystack][Gao2025UNIAH] remind us benchmarks themselves are still evolving: RULER/LongBench assume uniform haystacks, while real agentic distractors are biased — a crack that none of the four camps has fully resolved.8:T70c,Given head-to-head 2024–2025 evidence, the 2026 choice is nearly unique. New pretrains: RoPE base ≥500000, 6-stage curriculum (8K→16K→32K→64K→128K→256K) at ~5% tokens/stage with mixed short–long data; a direct reproduction of Dubey et al. [Dubey2024Llama3] and Xiong et al. [Xiong2023LongLlama]. Retrofit 32K–128K: YaRN + 400 FT steps; the inference stack must support per-dim ramp and attention temperature 1/√(1+log s) [Peng2023YaRN]; stack LongLoRA [Chen2023LongLoRA] on top if budget is tight — it runs on one 8×A100 node. Retrofit 512K–2M: LongRoPE evolutionary search over 96 dims + 1000 FT steps; budget ~10% of training for search because Ding et al. [Ding2024LongRoPE] show the searched non-uniform pattern cannot be replicated by smooth formulas. Zero-shot ≤2× extension: NTK-aware [bloc972023NTK] beats PI but is not production-grade.

Evaluation trio: RULER [Hsieh2024RULER] (13 synthetic tasks giving capacity ceiling), LongBench [Bai2023LongBench] (real downstream, blocks the 'retrieves but doesn't reason' failure mode), and the heterogeneous haystack setup from Li et al. [Li2025Haystack] (degradation under biased distractors). Stop reporting PPL as a long-context metric — with sparse positional signal, PPL can look great while downstream collapses.

Monitor two things. First, attention sinks: long-context inference concentrates mass on the first few tokens; a sudden rise in sink share together with a drop in RULER multi-hop usually means the ramp's frequency band is mis-set.
