16:T51d,By 2026 the parallelism decision for frontier LLM training is no longer serial ('DP first, then TP, then PP'); it is a joint sharding on a single equation where memory and compute are both dominated by 6ND (params × tokens) and 4BL²HL_layer (attention activations). The correct recipe maps mesh dimensions onto physical topology by **communication-primitive frequency**: TP's per-layer all-reduce and EP's all-to-all live inside the 8-GPU NVLink island [Shoeybi2019Megatron, GShard2020]; PP's per-microbatch P2P may cross 400 Gbps IB [Narayanan2021PTD]; DP/FSDP stretches across pods [Zhao2023FSDP]. Above that shared floor four camps argue: hand-written 4D (MegaScale [Jiang2024MegaScale]), auto-parallel (Alpa / GSPMD [Zheng2022Alpa, Xu2021GSPMD]), FSDP-only [Rajbhandari2021ZeROInfinity], and a conservative 3D+optional-SP line [Grattafiori2024Llama3]. This report reorganises the 50+ pieces of evidence from the anchor set and the extended pool onto that comparison, delivers an executable mesh-selection checklist for 2026-era H100/B200 clusters, traces the PP-schedule upgrade path to zero-bubble 1F1B [Qi2023ZeroBubble], specifies when long-sequence CP becomes mandatory (L ≥ 32K [Liu2023RingAttn, Jacobs2023Ulysses, Fang2024USP]), and flags the missing 100B+ public reproduction for auto-parallel.17:T470,By 2026 the 4D-parallelism decision is no longer a serial waterfall of 'DP→TP→PP'; it is a joint sharding on 6ND + 4BL²HL_layer. Each mesh dimension must map onto physical topology by primitive frequency: TP's per-layer all-reduce [Shoeybi2019Megatron] and EP's all-to-all [GShard2020] on the 8-GPU NVLink island, PP's per-microbatch P2P [Narayanan2021PTD] across 400 Gbps IB, DP/FSDP [Rajbhandari2021ZeROInfinity] across pods. PP scheduling defaults to zero-bubble 1F1B [Qi2023ZeroBubble]; SP [Korthikanti2022SP] and FP8 [Fishman2024FP8Trillion] are always on; CP is added for L ≥ 32K [Liu2023RingAttn, Jacobs2023Ulysses, Fang2024USP]. Among the four camps, hand-tuned 4D [Jiang2024MegaScale] remains the only publicly reproducible recipe at 100B+; auto-parallel [Zheng2022Alpa, Xu2021GSPMD] reaches 90–95% of hand-tuned below 100B but has no public 100B+ evidence; FSDP-only tops out near 70B; the 3D+optional-SP camp [Grattafiori2024Llama3] still works for dense but must cede to EP under MoE [DeepSeek2024V3]. The 2026 sane MFU bands are 40–55% dense and 25–45% MoE; numbers below those bands usually mean a wrong mesh.18:T5d4,Start from the bookkeeping. A transformer layer's memory/compute is dominated by two terms: parameters plus optimiser state (≈ 6ND, with N parameters and D tokens) and activations (leading term 4BL²HL_layer, where B is batch, L sequence, H hidden and L_layer depth). These two terms decide which axis saturates first. The 6ND term is compressed jointly by DP/FSDP sharding and TP weight shards; the 4BL²HL_layer term is amortised by SP (which slices LayerNorm/Dropout activations) [Korthikanti2022SP] and by CP (which slices the K/V axis of attention) [Liu2023RingAttn, Jacobs2023Ulysses]. Megatron [Shoeybi2019Megatron] encodes TP's communication as 'one all-reduce per forward, one per backward'—this per-layer primitive is precisely why TP cannot cross the NVLink island. PTD-P [Narayanan2021PTD] then quantifies PP's per-microbatch P2P cost and shows that PP over 400 Gbps IB does not hurt MFU. MegaScale [Jiang2024MegaScale] demonstrates this three-tier mapping (intra-NVLink TP / inter-node PP / cross-pod DP) on 10K+ H100 at dense-175B MFU 55.2%, making it the 2026 production baseline for mesh allocation. The complementary analytical work of Wu et al. [Wu2023Rethinking] [Wu2023Rethinking] plots the memory/comm Pareto frontier and gives a concrete numerical answer to questions like 'should TP actually max out at 8?'. The consensus of this section: **parallel axes are variables in the equation, not a function of model size**—write the equation first, then slice the mesh.19:T5fd,The same parallel axes can differ by 2× MFU depending on physical placement. The allocation rule is not 'put the largest axis outermost' but **put the highest-frequency primitive innermost**. TP issues two all-reduces per layer [Shoeybi2019Megatron], each of order ~2·(h²/TP) bytes—only NVLink (900 GB/s on H100, 1.8 TB/s on B200) can absorb it; crossing IB collapses TP. EP's all-to-all [GShard2020] is analogous, twice per MoE layer; DeepSeek-V3 [DeepSeek2024V3] specifically wrote an NVLink-domain all-to-all kernel to push MoE into the 25–45% MFU band. PP's P2P [Narayanan2021PTD] is one activation transfer per microbatch—small and infrequent, easily served by 400 Gbps IB. DP/FSDP's all-gather/reduce-scatter [Rajbhandari2021ZeROInfinity, Wang2023ZeROpp] fire once per step and tolerate cross-pod latency. Rank the four primitives by frequency and the mesh ordering (inner → outer) follows mechanically: TP/EP → PP → DP/FSDP. The public recipes of MegaScale [Jiang2024MegaScale] and Llama 3 [Grattafiori2024Llama3] obey this order. Counter-examples? The FSDP-only route [Rajbhandari2021ZeROInfinity] collapses all primitives into cross-pod all-gather and assumes NVMe bandwidth compensates—untenable above 70B. In the auto-parallel route, Alpa [Zheng2022Alpa] and GSPMD [Xu2021GSPMD] can in principle derive this ordering from a cost model, but public GPU evidence stops at <100B and no one has filled the 100B+ gap. The following table aligns the four primitives by frequency, volume and topology requirement:1a:T65f,PP bubbles come from backward dependency chains, not from communication. PipeDream [Harlap2018PipeDream] first attacked them with async updates, but frontier training abandoned that route due to accuracy drift. Narayanan et al.'s interleaved 1F1B [Narayanan2021PTD] shrinks the synchronous bubble from (P-1)/M to (P-1)/(v·M), typically v=2, at the cost of v× activation memory. Qi et al. [Qi2023ZeroBubble] observed that backward splits into B (input-grad) and W (weight-grad): only B has a pipeline dependency, W does not. By deferring W into the bubble, ZB-H2 drives the synchronous bubble to zero exactly—this is precise scheduling, not an approximation. Frontier stacks (Megatron-LM, NeMo, MegaScale) have defaulted to ZB since 2024, adding 2–5 pp MFU over interleaved 1F1B. Qi et al. [Qi2024ControllableMem] then rewrote schedules as repetitions of a building block, exposing activation lifespan as the main memory variable and placing zero-bubble and 1F1B on the same design manifold—a theoretical entry point for memory-aware scheduling on 80 GB HBM B200. Heterogeneous models (MoE + dense) are not fully covered by ZB yet; AdaPtis [Guo2025AdaPtis] fills the gap by jointly optimising partition+placement+schedule. PipeFill [Arfeen2024PipeFill] takes a different angle: if bubbles cannot be eliminated, let them run other jobs; a useful patch in multi-tenant clusters but not a challenge to ZB as the frontier default. **An executable checklist**: if the team has ZB infrastructure → go ZB-H2; if not and M/P ≥ 4 → interleaved 1F1B with v=2; for heterogeneous MoE → evaluate AdaPtis-style adaptive schedules.1b:T667,L 从 8K 涨到 128K，4BL²HL_layer 从次要项变成主导项；这时 TP/PP/DP 的 4GB ≈ 300GB 级调优全部失效，问题变成 'attention 激活怎么在 device 间摊开'。两条路线：(1) SP [Li2021SP, Korthikanti2022SP] 把 LayerNorm/Dropout 这类 no-op 的 activation 沿 sequence 维切，TP 的 all-reduce 被替换成 all-gather + reduce-scatter，通讯量不变——这是 'TP 的免费扩展'，应该默认开。(2) CP 专攻 attention 本身：Ring Attention [Liu2023RingAttn] 把 KV block 在 device 圈上 ring 式传，与本地 attention 重叠，L 近似线性扩；DeepSpeed Ulysses [Jacobs2023Ulysses] 反过来用 all-to-all 在 sequence 与 head 两个轴间切换，head 数充足时更均衡。争论在于：head 不够（GQA 把 KV head 压到 8）时 Ulysses 失效，这时 Ring 占优；head 充足（72B 类 128 head）时 Ulysses 的 all-to-all 比 Ring 的多跳传递低延迟。Fang et al. Two routes: (1) SP [Li2021SP, Korthikanti2022SP] slices no-op LayerNorm/Dropout activations along the sequence axis, replacing TP's all-reduce with all-gather + reduce-scatter at equal communication volume—this is TP's free extension and should be on by default. (2) CP targets attention itself: Ring Attention [Liu2023RingAttn] passes KV blocks around a ring of devices, overlapping local attention to let L scale nearly linearly; DeepSpeed Ulysses [Jacobs2023Ulysses] instead uses all-to-all to swap between sequence and head axes, which is more balanced when heads are abundant. The argument: when heads are scarce (GQA drops KV heads to 8) Ulysses degrades and Ring wins; when heads are abundant (72B-class with 128 heads) Ulysses's all-to-all beats Ring's multi-hop traffic in latency. Fang et al. [Fang2024USP] unify the two under topology-aware selection driven by NVLink/IB ratio and head count—the 2026 de facto standard. StarTrail [Liu2024StarTrail] adds concentric rings to remove the head-count ceiling. DistFlashAttn [Li2023DistFlashAttn] supplies causal-mask load balancing; without it the first half of devices sit idle while the second half congests and CP fails to achieve linear speedup. HelixPipe [Zhang2025HelixPipe] couples CP with PP, specifically addressing the bubble expansion caused by quadratic attention. InternEvo [Chen2024InternEvo] provides industrial cross-validation. The consensus here: **for L ≥ 32K, CP is not optional, it is required**; mixing Ring with Ulysses is decided by head count and NVLink topology.1d:T5cc,Now bind the technical points to each camp's living space. Camp A (hand-tuned 4D), led by MegaScale [Jiang2024MegaScale], DeepSeek-V3 [DeepSeek2024V3] and Llama 3 405B [Grattafiori2024Llama3], remains the only route with public MFU numbers at 100B+; its downside is high engineering cost and slow response to heterogeneous new models (dynamic MoE, multimodal). Camp B (auto-parallel), supported by Alpa [Zheng2022Alpa], GSPMD [Xu2021GSPMD], OneFlow SBP [Yuan2021OneFlow], TAPAS [Shi2023TAPAS] and Learning-to-Shard [Yin2025RLShard], has strong evidence on GPUs below 100B and on the full TPU stack (PaLM 540B), but no public GPU 100B+ reproduction. Camp C (FSDP-only), anchored by ZeRO-Infinity [Rajbhandari2021ZeROInfinity], ZeRO++ [Wang2023ZeROpp], AMSP [Chen2023AMSP] and SDP4Bit [Jia2024SDP4Bit], offers minimal code; but past 70B, HBM↔NVMe bandwidth kills it. Camp D (3D with optional SP), flagshipped by LLaMA [Touvron2023LLaMA] and Llama 3 [Grattafiori2024Llama3], still works for dense but cedes to EP under MoE [GShard2020, DeepSeek2024V3]. A pragmatic reading: **Camp A is the mandatory path for frontier; Camp B is an engineering accelerator below 100B; Camp C is a lean default at ≤70B dense; Camp D has been pushed out of the frontier centre by the combined pressure of L ≥ 32K and MoE**. This is not hedging—it is a survival region mapped by four coordinates (N, D, L, is-MoE). **Do**
1. 先算 N、D、L 三个数，把 6ND + 4BL²HL_layer 的占比分解出来。attention 占比 > 30% 时 SP 必开 [Korthikanti2022SP]；> 50% 时 CP 必开 [Liu2023RingAttn, Jacobs2023Ulysses, Fang2024USP]。
2. Mesh 分配按通讯原语频次排序（内→外）：TP/EP → PP → DP/FSDP [Shoeybi2019Megatron, Narayanan2021PTD, Rajbhandari2021ZeROInfinity]。

4. PP 调度默认切到 zero-bubble 1F1B [Qi2023ZeroBubble]；没有 ZB 基建且 M/P ≥ 4 时退回 interleaved 1F1B + v=2 [Narayanan2021PTD]。

2. Assign the mesh by primitive frequency (inner → outer): TP/EP → PP → DP/FSDP [Shoeybi2019Megatron, Narayanan2021PTD, Rajbhandari2021ZeROInfinity].
3. TP sizing heuristics: 7B → TP=1; 70B → TP=4; 100B+ → TP=8; 300B+ → TP=8 with PP ≥ 16 [Jiang2024MegaScale].
4. Default PP scheduling is zero-bubble 1F1B [Qi2023ZeroBubble]; fall back to interleaved 1F1B with v=2 [Narayanan2021PTD] only when ZB infrastructure is missing and M/P ≥ 4.
5. FP8 matmul is on by default [Fishman2024FP8Trillion]; FlashAttention is on by default [Dao2022FlashAttention].
6. For L ≥ 32K, let USP's topology-aware selector mix Ring [Liu2023RingAttn] and Ulysses [Jacobs2023Ulysses]; under causal masks, always run DistFlashAttn-style load balancing [Li2023DistFlashAttn].
7. Under MoE, lock EP inside the NVLink island [GShard2020, DeepSeek2024V3]; for heterogeneous layers evaluate AdaPtis-style adaptive schedules [Guo2025AdaPtis].
